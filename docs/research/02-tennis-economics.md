# Real tennis economics (2026-07-22)

Calibration data for the game's economy. Raw agent output: [raw/2026-07-22-market-research.json](raw/2026-07-22-market-research.json)

## Junior costs (the resource sink)

- Total beginner → pro: **$300–400k** (Sporting Intelligence; LTA ~$400k ages 5–18; Thiem discourse: "$1M to go pro").
- Ages 13–18: **$80–100k/year** at the elite end (Thiem's numbers).
- Academies: Rafa Nadal Academy **~€56–60k/yr**; IMG Academy **$88.9k/yr** (grades 10+); weekly camps ~€2.2k.
- Coaching: US average ~$113/hr; competitive junior lessons $75–200/hr; elite $150–300/hr; travel coach ~$250/day → $3k/week.
- Tournament travel: regional junior families **$5–25k/yr**; global schedules $50–150k/yr; entries $100–1,000/event.
- Equipment/stringing ~$10k/yr at high level; fitness $2.8–5k/yr.

## Prize money (the cliff)

| Level | Numbers |
|---|---|
| ITF M15/W15 | $20k total/event (2025); title ≈ $2–3k; R32 loser ≈ $100–160; W25 qualifying R1 as low as $50 |
| ITF M25/W35 | $30k total/event |
| Challenger | 2025 total $28.5M, 2026 $32.4M; CH50 title ≈ $8k, CH125 title ≈ $28k |
| ATP 250 | R1 loser ≈ $6.9k |
| ATP 1000 | Indian Wells R1 ≈ $17.6k, title $426k |
| Grand Slam | **R1 loser $80–110k** (US Open 2025: $110k); champion ~$2M+ |

Case study: Brenda Fruhvirtova won 8 ITF titles in 2022 → **$43,071 for the year**. Kiranpal Pannu 2022: $6,771 earned vs $34,500 spent.

## Break-even (the valley of death)

- Break-even ranking ≈ **ATP/WTA 150** (Schoettl et al., SSRN 2025 / ScienceDirect 2026).
- Touring costs: **$53–105k/yr** (USTA estimate $143k/yr with full team; ITF 2013: ~$39k solo).
- Only **~336 men and ~251 women** break even per year. Only 97 players worldwide earned >$1M prize money in 2022.
- Only 32–34% of top-100 juniors (2008 cohort) ended careers financially positive.

## Funding sources (mechanics with tradeoffs)

- **Federation grants**: LTA Pro Scholarship up to **£80k/yr** (16–24, performance-conditional, revocable); LTA transition £15–40k; USTA junior grants up to $9k.
- **ITF Grand Slam Player Development Programme**: $25–50k grants, developing nations (e.g. Alex Eala $25k); junior touring teams with coaches.
- **Equipment sponsorships (juniors)**: mostly product-only (racquets/strings/shoes, ~$1k+/yr value), 3–4 year terms; cash only for the very top (young Sharapova: $25–50k covering equipment+travel). Nike: "don't call us, we'll call you."
- **Income-share investors**: real practice – Alison Riske funded at 18 (ranked ~200) by a family friend for % of future earnings; modern retail versions: Fantium, Kyrgios-linked securities.

## Timeline & attrition (calibration)

- Start ~5, first ITF juniors ~13–14, first pro points ~17–18, **44–54 months** from first point to top 100, top-100 entry ~21.5–22, peak 23–28 (women ~23–24, men ~25–27), decline after ~29 (PLOS One ranking-history study).
- Only **~58%** of ITF junior top-10 boys ever reach ATP top 100; ~45% of top-20 junior girls reach WTA top 100; ~1 in 10,000 junior players cracks top 500.
- Gender asymmetry: women's ITF tour has more total prize money + free hospitality at W50+ (28,000 free hotel nights in 2025); women peak ~2 years earlier, shorter careers.
